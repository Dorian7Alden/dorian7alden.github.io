Now, very important pronunciation rule, it's for cancellation and it works especially with American English.

There are three strong sounds, the strong sounds are the "S, N and L". 

And there are three weak sounds. The weak sounds are  "d, t, and th".

This means if there is "d, t and th" next to "S, N and L", many times this sound is canceled, so you know the expression. "You and I". We don't say "You andie", we say "you an die".

The "n" cancels the "d", and we connect the /n/ sound.
